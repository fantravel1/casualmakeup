Makeup routines that respect your time

From "I have 3 minutes" to "I want to look polished" — every routine is tested, timed, and built for real mornings.

3 min

The 3-Minute Face

The absolute essentials. For mornings when you overslept but still want to look human.

1

Tinted Moisturizer or Sunscreen

Apply with fingers, blend quickly across the face. Skip foundation entirely — this is about even-ing out your skin tone in seconds, not full coverage.

45 seconds
Budget: CeraVe Tinted SPF 30 Mid: Laura Mercier Tinted Moisturizer Splurge: Chanel Les Beiges
2

Concealer

Under eyes only. Tap gently with your ring finger (least pressure = most natural blend). This step makes you look awake even when you're not.

30 seconds
Budget: Maybelline Instant Age Rewind Mid: NARS Radiant Creamy Splurge: Cle de Peau
3

Mascara + Tinted Lip Balm

One quick coat of mascara on upper lashes. Swipe on a tinted lip balm. That's it. You're done. These two products do 80% of the "she looks put together" heavy lifting.

45 seconds
Budget: Essence Lash Princess + Burt's Bees Tinted Mid: Glossier Lash Slick + Clinique Almost Lipstick

Quick Stats

  • Products: 3
  • Total cost (budget): ~$18
  • Total cost (mid): ~$55
  • Skill level: Absolute beginner
  • Best for: Running late, errands, gym recovery

Pro Tip

Keep these 3 products in a small pouch by your door. The "grab and go" strategy means you can literally do this routine in the car (parked, obviously).

5 min

The 5-Minute Face

Our most popular routine. The sweet spot between effort and payoff. 5 products, 5 steps, done.

1

Tinted Moisturizer

Dot on forehead, cheeks, nose, chin. Blend outward with fingers for the most natural finish. Use a damp sponge if you want slightly more coverage.

60 seconds
2

Concealer

Under eyes and any blemishes. Apply in an inverted triangle shape under each eye. Tap to blend with ring finger for a natural, brightening effect.

45 seconds
3

Cream Blush

Smile naturally. Dab cream blush on the apples of your cheeks. Blend upward toward temples. This one step adds more life to your face than any other product.

30 seconds
4

Mascara

One coat on upper lashes only. Wiggle the wand from root to tip for maximum lift without clumps. Skip lower lashes for a more casual, fresh look.

45 seconds
5

Brow Gel + Lip Tint

Brush brows upward with a clear or tinted brow gel. Swipe on a lip tint or tinted balm. These two quick steps frame your face and tie the whole look together.

60 seconds

Quick Stats

  • Products: 5
  • Total cost (budget): ~$28
  • Total cost (mid): ~$85
  • Skill level: Beginner
  • Best for: Work, school, casual dates, daily wear

Why This Works

The 5-Minute Face covers the 5 zones that matter most: skin, under-eyes, cheeks, lashes, and lips. These 5 products create the "she just looks good" effect without anyone being able to tell what you did.

7 min

The 7-Minute Face

A little extra polish. When you have a few more minutes and want that "I tried but not too hard" look.

1

Primer or SPF

Thin layer across face. Let it set for 15 seconds before moving to step 2. This creates a smooth canvas and helps everything last longer.

30 seconds
2

Tinted Moisturizer

Blend with a damp sponge for smoother, more even coverage. The sponge picks up excess and gives a more skin-like finish than fingers alone.

60 seconds
3

Concealer + Light Set

Under eyes, around nose, chin. Light dusting of translucent powder on T-zone only. This prevents shine without looking cakey.

60 seconds
4

Cream Blush + Bronzer

Blush on cheeks for color. Touch of bronzer on temples and along the jawline for warmth and dimension. This step adds structure to the face.

60 seconds
5

Brows

Fill sparse areas with a brow pencil or pomade. Set with clear or tinted brow gel. Well-groomed brows frame the entire face.

60 seconds
6

Mascara + Lips

Two coats of mascara for more definition. Apply lip liner for shape, then your lip color of choice. A more finished look in under a minute.

60 seconds

Quick Stats

  • Products: 7-8
  • Total cost (budget): ~$40
  • Total cost (mid): ~$120
  • Skill level: Intermediate
  • Best for: Presentations, brunches, photo days
10 min

The 10-Minute Face

The full casual face. Maximum polish, still under 10 minutes. For days when you want to feel your best.

1

Skincare Prep

Moisturizer + SPF. Let absorb while you lay out your products. This prep step makes everything apply smoother and last longer throughout the day.

90 seconds
2

Primer + Base

Primer on T-zone for longevity. Tinted moisturizer or light foundation blended with a damp beauty sponge for a flawless, second-skin finish.

90 seconds
3

Concealer + Setting

Precise concealer placement under eyes and on any areas that need extra coverage. Set with a light dusting of translucent powder to lock everything in.

60 seconds
4

Eyes — Shadow + Liner

One wash of neutral shadow across the lid. Tight-line the upper lash line with a pencil or gel liner for subtle definition without the "done up" look.

90 seconds
5

Blush + Bronzer + Highlight

Cream blush on cheeks, bronzer on the hollows, a dab of highlight on the cheekbones and inner corner of eyes. This adds dimension and radiance.

60 seconds
6

Brows

Fill, shape, and set your brows. This is the face-framing step that pulls the whole look together. Use short, hair-like strokes for the most natural result.

60 seconds
7

Mascara + Lips + Setting Spray

Two coats of mascara for full, lifted lashes. Lip color of your choice. Lock everything in place with a setting spray — two spritzes in an X pattern.

60 seconds

Quick Stats

  • Products: 10-12
  • Total cost (budget): ~$55
  • Total cost (mid): ~$165
  • Skill level: Intermediate
  • Best for: Date nights, events, important meetings, feeling yourself

Remember

Even the 10-minute routine is still "casual." It's polished, not glam. The goal is enhanced skin, not transformed skin. You should still look like you — just the best version.
