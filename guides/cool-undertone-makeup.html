Undertone Guide

The complete guide to makeup for cool undertones

Your best shades in foundation, blush, lips, and eyeshadow — plus the colors to skip and products at every price point.

12 min read • Updated Feb 2026

Quick Answer

If you have cool undertones, your best makeup shades lean pink, rose, berry, mauve, and blue-red. Choose foundations with pink or neutral-cool bases, blushes in soft pink or plum, eyeshadows in mauve, taupe, and silver, and lip colors in berry, dusty rose, or blue-based red. Avoid orange-toned products and warm yellow-based shades, which can make cool skin look sallow or muddy.

How to confirm cool undertones

Before building your color palette, make sure you're actually cool-toned. Undertone is different from skin depth — you can have cool undertones whether your skin is fair, medium, or deep. Here are the three most reliable tests:

  • Vein check — Look at the veins on the inside of your wrist in natural light. Blue or purple veins indicate cool undertones. Green veins suggest warm, and a mix means neutral.
  • Jewelry test — Silver jewelry flatters cool undertones more than gold. If silver makes your skin glow while gold looks slightly off, you're likely cool-toned.
  • Color draping — Hold a pure white shirt and an off-white or cream shirt near your face. If pure white brightens your complexion and cream washes you out, cool undertones are confirmed.

If all three tests point the same direction, you can shop with confidence. If results are mixed, you may be neutral-cool — which means you have flexibility with both cool and some muted warm shades.

Cool-toned shades in pink, berry, and mauve are your best friends.

Your cool undertone color palette

Once you know you're cool-toned, choosing the right shades becomes straightforward. Here's your complete color guide by product category:

1

Rose-toned foundations

Look for foundations described as "cool," "pink," or "rose" in the shade name. Avoid anything labeled "warm," "golden," or "olive." The right foundation should disappear into your jawline — if it looks yellow or orange on your skin, the undertone is wrong.

Budget: Maybelline Fit Me Dewy + Smooth in cool shades (~$9) Mid: NARS Sheer Glow in cool/neutral shades (~$49) Premium: Armani Luminous Silk in pink-based shades (~$69)
2

Pink-based blushes

Cool undertones come alive with pink, mauve, plum, and cool berry blushes. These shades mimic the natural flush your skin produces. Apply to the apples of your cheeks and blend upward toward the temples for a lifted look.

Budget: e.l.f. Putty Blush in Bali (~$7) Mid: Rare Beauty Soft Pinch in Grace or Hope (~$23) Premium: Clinique Cheek Pop in Pink Pop (~$30)
3

Mauve and taupe eyeshadows

Cool undertones are beautifully complemented by mauve, taupe, cool brown, silver, slate, and plum eyeshadows. For a simple everyday look, sweep a soft taupe across the lid and add a deeper mauve to the crease. Skip warm coppers and orangey browns.

Budget: NYX Ultimate Shadow Palette in Cool Neutrals (~$18) Mid: Tarte Tartelette in Bloom (cool-toned shades) (~$39) Premium: Charlotte Tilbury Luxury Palette in The Sophisticate (~$55)
4

Berry lip tones

Berry, mauve, dusty rose, plum, and blue-based reds are the standout lip colors for cool undertones. For casual everyday wear, a sheer berry balm or mauve tinted lip oil looks effortlessly polished. For evenings, a classic blue-red lipstick is timeless on cool skin.

Budget: Burt's Bees Tinted Lip Balm in Rose (~$7) Mid: MAC Matte Lipstick in Mehr or Twig (~$23) Premium: Pat McGrath MatteTrance in Elson (~$39)

Colors to avoid

Wearing the wrong undertone doesn't look terrible — it just makes your skin look slightly off. Here are the shades that work against cool undertones:

  • Orange-toned products — Orange blushes, coral lipsticks, and apricot eyeshadows clash with pink undertones and can make skin look muddy or sallow
  • Warm yellows and golds — Yellow-based foundations, warm gold highlighters, and mustard eyeshadows pull your complexion toward an unnatural warmth
  • Warm bronzers — Orange or yellow-based bronzers look artificial on cool skin. If you want warmth, choose a cool-toned or neutral bronzer with a taupe or rosy base instead
  • Warm nude lips — Caramel, honey, and peach-toned nudes can make cool skin look washed out. Choose pink-based nudes or mauve tones instead
  • Copper and warm brown eyeshadows — These warm-leaning neutrals can make your eyes look tired. Stick to cool browns and taupes

Product recommendations by budget

Building a cool-toned makeup kit doesn't require a large budget. Here are the essentials at three price points:

  1. Budget (under $15 per product) — Maybelline Fit Me foundation in a cool shade, e.l.f. Putty Blush in a pink tone, NYX Butter Gloss in a berry shade, and ColourPop eyeshadow singles in taupe and mauve
  2. Mid-range ($15–$40 per product) — Rare Beauty foundation in a cool shade, Rare Beauty Soft Pinch blush, MAC lipstick in a mauve or berry, and Tarte eyeshadow palette with cool neutrals
  3. Premium ($40+ per product) — Armani Luminous Silk foundation, Clinique Cheek Pop blush, Pat McGrath lipstick, and Charlotte Tilbury eyeshadow quad in cool tones

Mix and match across price points — splurge where it matters most to you and save on products you use less frequently.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between cool and neutral undertones?

Cool undertones have pink, red, or blue hues beneath the skin's surface, while neutral undertones are a balanced mix of both warm and cool. The easiest test: look at the veins on your inner wrist. Blue or purple veins indicate cool undertones, while green veins suggest warm. If you see a mix of both, you're likely neutral. Neutral undertones can wear both warm and cool shades comfortably.

Can cool undertones wear bronzer?

Yes, cool undertones can absolutely wear bronzer — the key is choosing the right shade. Avoid orange-toned or overly warm bronzers. Instead, look for cool-toned or neutral bronzers with a slightly rosy or taupe base. Apply lightly to the hollows of your cheeks, temples, and jawline for a natural contour without clashing with your undertone.

What are the best lip colors for cool skin undertones?

Cool undertones look stunning in berry shades, blue-based reds, mauves, plums, and rosy pinks. For everyday casual looks, try a mauve or dusty rose lip. For bolder moments, a classic blue-red or deep berry is universally flattering on cool skin. Avoid orange-based corals and warm nude shades that can make cool skin look sallow.

How do I avoid looking washed out with cool undertone makeup?

The biggest mistake is choosing shades that are too pale or too ashy. Add dimension with a cool-toned blush in soft pink or mauve, and use a berry or rosy lip color to bring life to the face. Avoid heavy powder that flattens your complexion. A touch of champagne or icy pink highlighter on the cheekbones also prevents a washed-out look without adding warmth.
