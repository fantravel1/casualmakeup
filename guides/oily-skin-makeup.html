Skin Type Guide

The complete guide to makeup for oily skin

Oil-free routines, mattifying techniques, and products that stay put from morning to night. No caking. No sliding. No midday meltdown.

15 min read • Updated Feb 2026

Quick Answer

The best casual makeup approach for oily skin uses oil-free tinted moisturizer, mattifying primer on the T-zone only, lightweight concealer, and a setting spray. Set with translucent powder on oily areas only — never the whole face. Use blotting papers midday instead of reapplying powder. The full routine takes 5–7 minutes.

Why oily skin needs a different approach

Oily skin produces excess sebum, which means makeup can slide, separate, and look shiny within hours. The biggest mistake? Piling on heavy, matte products to "control" oil. This triggers your skin to produce more oil to compensate.

The casual makeup approach for oily skin is counterintuitive: use lightweight, hydrating formulas, set strategically, and let your skin's natural glow work for you.

The right products make all the difference for oily skin.

The 5-minute oily skin routine

This routine is specifically designed for oily and combination skin types. Every product is chosen for its staying power and oil-control properties.

1

Mattifying primer (T-zone only)

Apply a pea-sized amount to your forehead, nose, and chin. Don't prime your whole face — your cheeks need some natural moisture. Let it set for 30 seconds.

Budget: e.l.f. Power Grip Primer (~$10) Mid: Milk Makeup Hydro Grip (~$36)
2

Oil-free tinted moisturizer

Use an oil-free formula with SPF. Apply with fingers — the warmth helps it blend into skin without sitting on top. Water-based formulas grip to skin better on oily types.

Budget: CeraVe Hydrating Tinted SPF (~$16) Mid: bareMinerals Complexion Rescue (~$38)
3

Lightweight concealer + powder set

Dab concealer under eyes and on blemishes. Set with a tiny amount of translucent powder on T-zone only. Use a fluffy brush and tap off excess before applying.

Budget: Maybelline Fit Me Loose Powder (~$8) Mid: Laura Mercier Translucent Powder (~$43)
4

Cream blush + mascara

Yes, cream blush works on oily skin. Apply on the apples of cheeks (which are usually less oily) and it melts into skin beautifully. One coat of waterproof mascara.

5

Setting spray

This is non-negotiable for oily skin. Two spritzes in an X pattern. A good setting spray extends wear by 4–6 hours and prevents the midday meltdown.

Budget: NYX Matte Finish Setting Spray (~$9) Mid: Urban Decay All Nighter (~$36)

Products to avoid with oily skin

  • Heavy, oil-based foundations — They'll slide off within hours
  • Thick cream foundations — Too heavy, will separate on oily areas
  • Shimmer highlighters on T-zone — Your skin already highlights itself
  • Excessive powder — Triggers more oil production and looks cakey
  • Silicone-heavy primers on whole face — Can cause product pilling

The midday refresh technique

Around 4–5 hours in, oily skin starts to show. Here's how to refresh without starting over:

  1. Blot your T-zone with blotting papers (press, don't rub)
  2. Mist with setting spray from arm's length
  3. If needed, tap a tiny bit of translucent powder on nose only
  4. Reapply lip product

This 60-second refresh extends your morning routine through the entire day.

Ingredients to look for

When shopping for oily skin makeup, these ingredients are your allies:

  • Niacinamide — Reduces oil production and minimizes pores
  • Salicylic acid — Helps prevent breakouts while wearing makeup
  • Zinc — Natural oil absorber and anti-inflammatory
  • Hyaluronic acid — Hydrates without adding oil (yes, oily skin needs hydration)
  • Kaolin clay — Gently absorbs excess oil in primers and powders

Frequently asked questions

What is the best type of foundation for oily skin?

Oil-free tinted moisturizers and water-based foundations work best for oily skin. Avoid heavy, oil-based formulas. Look for ingredients like niacinamide, salicylic acid, or zinc which help control oil production. Mattifying primers on the T-zone extend wear time significantly.

How do I stop my makeup from sliding off oily skin?

Three key steps: 1) Use a mattifying primer on your T-zone before base makeup. 2) Set only oily areas with a light dusting of translucent powder — avoid powdering your whole face. 3) Finish with a setting spray. During the day, use blotting papers instead of adding more powder.

Should I use powder or cream products on oily skin?

Contrary to popular belief, cream products can work beautifully on oily skin when set properly. Cream blush and concealer blend more naturally. The key is setting cream products with a light translucent powder on the T-zone only. Avoid heavy powder all over, which can trigger more oil production.

How often should I blot oily skin during the day?

Blot as needed, typically every 2–3 hours. Use blotting papers or a clean tissue — never add more powder on top of existing makeup, as this creates a cakey buildup. Press gently, don't rub. Some women find that a setting spray midday refreshes makeup without the need for blotting.
